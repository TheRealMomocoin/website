Mondays are horrible!

Monday mornings have a dubious reputation, and not without reason. For many people, it marks the end of the weekend - that precious time often spent relaxing, enjoying hobbies and family. Monday morning, on the other hand, brings the alarm clock that rings too early, the stress of the working week and the feeling that the next weekend is miles away.

Returning to everyday life is difficult, especially after two days of freedom. The change from “I do what I feel like doing” to “I have to work again” triggers an inner defensive attitude in many people. This is often compounded by tiredness, whether due to a lack of sleep or simply the psychological pressure to get the week off to a successful start.

Mondays also have a bad reputation in society. Phrases such as “I have the Monday blues” or “This is a typical Monday” reinforce the feeling that Mondays are fundamentally bad - a self-fulfilling prophecy that makes it even harder to like them.
